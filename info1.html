Infographic: Understanding Consent in Relationships

The infographic aims to provide comprehensive information about consent in relationships, emphasizing its significance in fostering healthy and respectful interactions.

Key Points

Definition of Consent:

Consent is a voluntary, enthusiastic, and clear agreement between all parties involved in any interaction. It's an ongoing process that requires active communication and mutual understanding.

Consent is:

  • Ongoing: Anyone can change their mind about what they’re interested in doing, anytime
  • Freely given: Saying ‘yes’ without pressure or manipulation
  • Specific: Saying ‘yes’ to one act doesn’t mean you’ve said ‘yes’ to others
  • Informed: Not deceiving or lying. For example, if someone says they’ll use a condom and then they don’t, there isn’t consent.
  • Enthusiastic: It’s about wanting to do something, not feeling like you have to or should do something.

Importance of Consent:

Consent is the cornerstone of healthy relationships. It promotes mutual respect, trust, and emotional well-being. Without consent, interactions can be harmful and damaging to individuals' physical and mental health.

Consent is essential:

  • Asking with words is often the best way to get clear consent
  • Really listen! Anything other than a ‘heck yes!’ is a ‘no’
  • Make space for a ‘no’. You don’t have to be excited to hear ‘no’. Using anger, pressure, or bargaining to get a ‘yes’ is not consent.
  • ‘Yes’ can change to ‘no’ at any time.

The infographic concludes by emphasizing that understanding and respecting consent is essential for building healthy, respectful, and fulfilling relationships. By promoting a culture of consent, individuals can create safer and more supportive environments where everyone's boundaries are valued and respected.
